## Design Context

### Users
TajerPro/KasserPro serves a mixed audience of cashiers, store owners, and managers, and design decisions should support all three without privileging one role at the expense of the others. Cashiers need speed, glanceability, and error-forgiving flows during repeated daily transactions. Store owners and managers need confidence, oversight, clarity, and a sense that the system is reliable enough for business-critical operations. The primary job to be done is helping retail teams complete sales, manage inventory, monitor performance, and operate the business efficiently in Arabic- and English-friendly workflows for the Egyptian market.

### Brand Personality
The brand personality is Professional, Premium, and Calm. The interface should feel trustworthy, composed, polished, and operationally serious rather than playful or trendy. The emotional target is a premium calm: smooth and modern enough to feel high quality, but steady and readable enough to support long work sessions and fast transactional use.

### Aesthetic Direction
The current product direction already points toward a warm, refined, light-first visual language with premium surfaces, soft depth, glass and frost treatments, and a functional palette built around green, amber, and cyan accents. Both light and dark themes should be supported, with parity in usability and perceived polish. Typography should continue balancing Latin readability with strong Arabic support, using the existing display and Arabic font strategy as a base. Avoid generic startup visuals, novelty-heavy styling, visual clutter, or overly loud color combinations; the UI should look intentional, polished, and business-grade.

Because no direct references or anti-references were provided, future design work should infer references from high-quality POS, finance, and productivity software rather than consumer-social patterns.

### Design Principles
1. Optimize for operational clarity first. Important numbers, actions, statuses, and exceptions must be scannable within a second in both Arabic and English contexts.
2. Keep premium visuals subordinate to usability. Depth, gradients, glass, and motion should reinforce hierarchy and confidence, never slow the user down or reduce legibility.
3. Design equally for cashier speed and management confidence. Transaction flows should feel fast and forgiving, while reporting, settings, and admin screens should feel stable, orderly, and trustworthy.
4. Support dual-theme quality by default. Light and dark experiences should both feel intentional, with strong contrast, clear focus states, and no token-level drift between themes.
5. Meet accessible, inclusive standards. Target WCAG AA contrast and interaction quality, preserve keyboard usability, respect reduced-motion preferences, and avoid color-only communication for critical meaning.
